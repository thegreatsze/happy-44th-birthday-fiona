01DBS · Marina Bay
She Who Holds It All Together
Narrator
At DBS Bank, Marina Bay Financial Centre, there is an Executive Director who moves between strategy, operations, technology and partnerships with the ease of someone born to do exactly this.
Colleagues say
"Always three steps ahead. Sees everything. Makes it look effortless."
Her office walls hold a Wharton Huntsman degree and a framed SAT score — 1600 out of 1600, perfect — tucked modestly behind a plant. Most visitors never notice. She never mentions it. (She also spent a semester at Tsinghua University in Beijing, which she mentions even less.)
But we know the truth...
Behind the Ice Princess is the warmest, most devoted wife and mother imaginable.

The Tennis Net Incident
She was young. She wanted to impress him. He played lots of tennis. She would clear the net — easily — and he would be amazed.
Narrator
Reader, she did not clear the net.
What actually happened
Fiona leapt with tremendous confidence. The net caught her. She landed — face first — on the other side.
Papa James (watching)
visible alarm 😰
Shiyao was terrified Papa James might be furious. He was not. Fiona got back up immediately — as she always does.
The moral

06Japan · The Family's Second Home
Chansu in Japan
A fun fact
The Chan family visits Japan so often that Japan has practically adopted them back.
In Japanese, "Chan"...
...means "cute little girl." Which, if you know Fiona Chan Su-Min, is almost offensively accurate. Petite. Precise. Undeniably a Chan.
And so the family calls her...
Chansu.
(Part of her name, Chan Su-Min. Entirely fitting.)
In Japan, amidst the sakura and perfectly organised train schedules, Fiona is completely at home. Perhaps because Japan, too, values order, beauty and quiet competence.

🪖 If you look carefully at the lantern, you'll spot Shiyao's old MINDEF dog tags. He hung them there so Japan would know he served too.

07Shiyao · Husband
The Difficult One
Narrator (with affection)
Shiyao is tempestuous. He has opinions — many, loud. He goes on rants. He starts arguments. He is, by his own admission, often a terrible example for the boys.
Shiyao (mid-rant)
"WHY is everything like THIS?! Nobody tells me ANYTHING!!"
Fiona (serene, patient)
...listens. ...continues listening. ...makes him his favourite food.
Underneath all of it: a man who loves her more than he knows how to say, and is terrified — every day — of not having enough time with her.

What I Should Have Said
Shiyao speaks
Shiyao
Fiona. I am sorry. For every outburst. For every argument I started for no good reason. For being tempestuous and difficult and a bad example for the boys.
Shiyao (continued)
You carry so much of this family. The mental load, the schedules, the patience — all of it. I see it. I have always seen it. I have not always said so.
And what I want you to know
You are more beautiful, more intelligent, and more kind than me in every way. I love you more than I know how to say. And every day I am afraid I will not have enough time with you.
Under all the noise and all the storms... this is the truth. It always has been.
